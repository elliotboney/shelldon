## What went well

1. **The LLM-free-core spine held under its hardest test.** Running `pytest` on *model-written code* — initiated from `core/` — was solved with `asyncio.create_subprocess_exec` + an `ast` import-scan, never an import. The loop, all tools, the gate, and quarantine all live worker-side or as subprocesses. The epic most likely to breach AD-1 didn't.
2. **Aggressive seam reuse kept each story small.** Credit gating = loop ceiling × the existing 5.2 cost-weighted budget (no new mechanism, no new field). Quarantine = the faces-registry single-writer-move pattern + the `learnings` atomic-UPSERT pattern. 9.4 tool promotions rode the 9.3 Approve/Deny keyboard verbatim. The relocation of `DEFAULT_WORKSPACE_ROOT` into `core/selfcode` avoided a core→worker import.
3. **Applied Epic 8's headline lesson directly.** Epic 8's retro: "fail-soft can mask a real defect indefinitely." 9.5's quarantine is the antidote — silent repeated tool-failure becomes a loud, bounded, self-healing action (strikes → move to `tools-quarantine/`).
4. **Scope discipline on 9.5.** ~15 deferred items had piled up; deciding the "pillars + cheap cleanup, network/shell policy → 9.6" split *before* writing the story (one batched owner question) stopped 9.5 from becoming a 3–4× monster.

## What was hard / recurring

1. **The dominant cost of the epic: incomplete fail-soft on escape/cleanup paths.** Every single story's review found 3–8 findings of the *same class* — `ProcessLookupError` from `kill()`, `CancelledError` slipping past `except Exception`, orphaned subprocesses / staged files, import-check gaps (`from shelldon import core`, dynamic `__import__`/`importlib.import_module`). `except Exception` is not enough where cancellation + subprocesses + untrusted code meet. The mechanism was usually right on the first pass; the *boundary/teardown* behavior was where bugs lived.
2. **Quarantine spanned a process boundary.** The worker detects failures but is ephemeral (can't accumulate a count); core decides + moves but doesn't run the tools. Resolved cleanly (worker reports `Result.tool_failures` → core strikes in sqlite → moves at threshold), but it took the most design thought of the epic.
